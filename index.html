Case Highlights

APPAREL
FRANCHISE

A master franchisor of a well known industrial apparel retail chain consisting of over 500 locations had a number of franchises that were suffering or failing. It was common for five to ten locations to be in distress each year mostly due to management failure. These locations were selected properly, the demographics were in their favor and all analysis suggested the troubled stores should be successful but they were not. In most cases the operator accumulated significant liabilities and it was beyond their ability to solve the problem alone. The operator was usually looking to sell in hopes of recovering their investment. However, inventories were not current and seriously over stocked. Obligations to suppliers were dramatically in arrears and in many cases the location lease was in jeopardy from being unable to pay their rent

The franchisor needed discreet intervention and an arms length approach which we were able to provide. The operator wanted out and to salvage something. No one wanted failure, litigation or public embarrassment.

We signed a network wide arrangement to address all problem locations as they occurred. We structured unique deep-discount arrangements with all apparel manufactures to eliminate the payable overhang. Using the excess and bloated inventories the operator liquidated noncurrent stock generating sufficient capital to retire the discounted debts. It was critical to also renegotiate the location leasing agreement to reduce the cost of the operations. The individual balance sheet and operating costs were now in a position allowing the franchisor to offer the location to another existing operator in the chain or to a new incoming franchisee.

SAVING THE DAY FOR MANY STAKE HOLDERS

For the franchisor they saved a location, protected their brand and avoided uncomfortable negotiations with their supply chain. The quasi arms length arrangement created discretion.

The operator was able to recover some value, protect their personal reputation and move out of a failing business efficiently and quickly.

The supply chain ended up with a new and successful partner/operator, eliminated the collection issues and improved their relationship with the franchisors supply channel.

Case Highlights

RESORT PROPERTY

Through a series of ownership changes and market issues a well known schedule “A” bank ended up the owner of a popular hotel and wellness property. Located in a national park the federal government was the land owner and lessor to the bank. The bank had hired a number of hot shot managers over a period of time with little success in managing the resort properly. In the midst of these management struggles a serious storm hit the whole region. Then lighting struck, literally. It knocked out the resorts control systems which in-turn damaged the main water supply check valve above the property. This immediately caused flooding on every floor and all guests had to be evacuated. The flooding persisted and began to erode portions of the resort and potentially causing a major landslide aimed towards a national highway and village.

We were already well known by the bank that owned the resort from other significant mandates they had hired us for. We received their emergency call asking us to come in. We quickly contacted our relationships within the construction industry and organized teams to take control. After working through the night, our contractors were able to repair the water main and stop the flooding. On day 2 it was time for us to take control of all the other issues on behalf of the bank. Top to bottom restoration was required. Our immediate focus was processing the insurance claims and making sure the environmental authorities were satisfied. The federal government was keenly involved and they needed to agree too a long term plan. At the same time the resorts accounts payables, vendors, tour operators and guest reservations all needed to be negotiated with and managed. Our team pulled out all stops to satisfy the stakeholders very quickly in a crisis situation.

The bank quietly gave us a mandate to find a longer term solution which was code for “sell this asset at the best possible price”. Skillfully, we negotiated a large settlement with the insurance company creating an outcome where the bank had minimal requirements to continue supporting the project. The property was completely restored, the federal government signed off on all environmental requirements and renewed the land lease for another 25 years. A professional resort ownership group was found and a sale closed before the following high season began. We were able to engage with the ultimate buyer during the reconstruction process allowing them to provide valuable input resulting in a much better outcome.

Case Highlights

LUXURY
MAGAZINE

This monthly publication targeted consumers interested in luxury home renovations, high end appliances, interior design and furnishings. The publication was subscription based and had a strong retail and news stand presence. It was majority owned by a private investor whose highly successful main business was in new development marketing and support for large scale developers. Although the publication was rich with high-end advertisers, prominent subscription levels and a solid circulation they continued to lose money with every edition. The operators who were minority shareholders continually sought further investment from the majority shareholder. Managing their accounts payable became a serious issue. Their vendors and the print houses were demanding upfront payment before each edition would be allowed to be published. It was time for help.

The first step was to quickly analyze the situation from within. We took a hands on approach and moved fast. We reviewed and matched all AR and AP, visited with the supply chain and discretely met with advertisers. Our analysis discovered that the advertising rates were inline with the market and the content to advertising ratio in the publication was appropriate. The cost of publishing and distribution was normal. Collections was not an issue but the add space utilization against the rate cards vs deposits didn’t add up. Something was not making sense and we had to look deeper. We discovered approximately 50% of the advertising revenue was converted to contra. The minority shareholders were accepting goods and services for the advertising space. This included leased vehicles, audio and visual systems, home furnishing and vacations. It’s not until you compare tear sheets with rate cards with the physical publication and the deposits that you can find the issues. The minority operators thought that contra deals were simply a normal course of business given they were taking low salaries. The investor felt deceived but had a long history with them so asked us to step in and mediate an exit.

As with many of our projects, we had no personal relationship with the investor or the operators so it was ideal for both parties to let us calmly handle the mediation. We then helped source new management from within the group and put processes and controls in place for the majority investor to continue with his investment confidently. Before wrapping up the project we restructured the cost of operations to where it became revenue positive. We then worked the phones with the companies long list of suppliers and negotiated deep discounts across their significant outstanding accounts payable. Eventually the publication was sold to a larger publication house and the investor was able to recover his investment with a gain.

SMALL CAP
PUBLIC COMPANY

A small cap (junior) public company specializing in adult content and sexual education hosting celebrities such as Dr. Ruth and other well known intimacy experts spent three years developing its platform as a resource portal. At the time, this was a new idea and did not involve pornography. As a junior public company, retail investors along with some institutional investors were key to capital formation and the ability to raise funding. Subscriptions and advertisers were equally difficult due to the stigma of the topic. As hard as they tried to explain their unique product, investors were not willing to touch such a controversial stock. The stock price declined in value which made raising capital even more difficult. Financial obligations mounted and the Board of Directors and founders contacted us to see what could be done.

We could not just simply deal with the financial obligations of the company without solving the fundamental issue of the content and focus of the website. We analyzed the market trends relying on our key strategic relationships that deliver results. We presented the Board with a comprehensive strategy which dealt with all the major issues. With the Boards consent and mandate we moved quickly. Step one was to rebrand and reposition the platform into a fresh new relationship and dating website. Next, we aligned the company with new market makers, PR agencies and promotional companies. It was important that all major stockholders enter into a selling restriction agreement allowing the company to focus on improving the business and not the markets. The rise in the stock was quick and sustainable. Now we could negotiate and settle all historic financial obligations using a much more stable and stronger stock as currency.

Advertising and subscriptions were now developing nicely. Institutional and retail investors returned to the story and the company was able to raise funding to grow the business. Eventually the site was sold to one of the larger online dating developers. Investors, founders, vendors, regulators, financiers and market makers ended up with a success story.
